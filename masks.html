Afika Face Masks are a local response to the current COVID-19 crises in South Africa.

We produce research grade, reusable face masks for the general public and general neccessary staff, such as security guards, petrol attendants, and shop assistants.

Afika is a non-profit project.

We have built a small scale support system for local seamstresses who are without income during the national lockdown. Local Afika saleswomen earn a marginal commission on each mask sold as a form of income.

Each Afika mask purchased supports the livelihood of these women in our community.

If you are a local, home-based seamstress who would like to become an Afika supplier, please contact us.

It is important to stress that the wearing of hand-made face masks will only offer limited protection.

They should not be considered sufficient protection against COVID-19. Additional preventative measures need to be adopted, such as:

Social distancing

Avoid non-essential direct contact with people who appear unwell and who have fever and cough. Stay inside the house as much as possible, and cook at home.

Cover your nose and mouth when coughing or sneezing

Use a tissue when possible and dispose of it prompty. If you don't have a tissue, cough into a flexed elbow.

Wash your hands frequently with soap and water

This will help reduce the spread of the virus from your hands to your face, or to other people.

Clean hard surfaces

Clean hard surfaces like door handles and table tops frequently, using a normal cleaning product.

Wear a mask

Wearing a mask that covers your nose and mouth reduces the risk of getting infected, or transmitting the infection to others.

Our mission is to #SlowTheSpread

Our mission is to equip as many South Africans with preventative measures as possible.
