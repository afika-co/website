safety tips to remember

It is important to stress that the wearing of hand-made face masks will only offer limited protection. They should not be considered sufficient protection against COVID-19. Additional preventative measures need to be adopted, such as:

Social distancing
‍Avoid non-essential direct contact with people who appear unwell and who have fever and cough. Stay inside the house as much as possible and cook at home.

Cover your nose and mouth when coughing or sneezing
‍Use a tissue when possible and dispose of it promptly. If you don't have a tissue, cough into a ﬂexed elbow.

Wash your hands frequently with soap and water
‍This will help reduce the spread of the virus from your hands to your face, or to other people.

Clean hard surfaces
‍Clean hard surfaces like door handles and table tops frequently using an everyday cleaning product.

Wear a mask
‍Wearing a mask that covers your nose and mouth reduces the risk of getting infected, or transmitting the infection to others.
