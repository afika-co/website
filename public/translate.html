We need to spread facts faster than the virus

Afika Access seeks to make the World Health Organisation (WHO) information about COVID-19 accessible to all people in South Africa.

What do we do?

Facts and stats about South African literacy aside, many people remain uneducated in our country. Conversations among public transport commuters, taxis, and in the hearts of South Africa's informal settlements reveal that a high level of ignorance and misinformation exists around COVID-19.

We are building a team of translators to translate COVID-19 information from the World Health Organisation into African languages.

Our primary initial focus is South African Languages. However, any African or other underrepresented language from around the globe is welcome to join!

We need to spread facts faster than the virus

How do we spread facts?

Once the information we have from WHO is translated, we aim to make short videos of a local speaker sharing the relevant information that we have translated.
